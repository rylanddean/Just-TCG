First launch

When you open Just TCG for the first time, the app loads its card catalog from the bundled seed data. This gives you immediate access to a recent snapshot of the Standard card pool without needing an internet connection.

The first time you have a connection, the app will silently refresh the catalog in the background to ensure you have the latest cards and format legality data.

Tip: If you want to verify the catalog is up to date, go to Settings → Data → Force network sync. The sync takes 10–30 seconds.

App tabs

Just TCG is organised around five tabs in the bottom navigation bar:

  • Home — Streak, activity heatmap, recent matches, Featured Deck
  • Decks — Your saved decks and deck builder
  • Cards — Browse and search the full Standard card catalog
  • Analytics — Matchup win rates, meta comparison, meta matchup score
  • Tournaments — Live tournament results and meta trends

Tournament Prep and Player Profiles are accessible from the Tournaments and Analytics tabs respectively, and from the Home screen navigation.

Build a deck

Create a new deck

1
Open the Decks tab and tap + in the top-right corner.
2
Enter a deck name and tap Create. The deck opens immediately in Deck Detail.
3
Tap Add Cards to open the card picker.

Adding cards

The card picker shows the full Standard card catalog in a grid. Tap any card to add one copy; tap again to increase the count. A blue badge shows how many copies are in the deck. Hold down on a card in the Cards tab to quick-add it to the most recently edited deck.

Use the search bar to find cards by name. Use the filter button (top right) to filter by type, subtype, set, HP range, retreat cost, weakness, or role tag.

  • Pokémon cards are capped at 4 copies (or 1 for cards that specify "you may only have 1"). Basic Energy is unlimited.
  • The deck builder only shows Standard-legal cards.
  • A legality badge at the top of the deck shows the current status: Valid, Invalid (with reason), or In progress.
Tip: The deck is automatically divided into sections — Pokémon (split by Basic/Stage 1/Stage 2), Trainer (Supporter/Item/Stadium/Tool), and Energy. Cards always appear in the correct section based on their subtype.

Deck status

Swipe left on a deck in the Decks list to change its status: Building, Playing, or Retired. Status is shown in the deck row and in the deck detail header. Retired decks are hidden by default but accessible via the filter control.

Import a deck

Just TCG can import any deck list in PTCGL copy-paste format.

Import from clipboard

1
In PTCGL, open the deck and tap Export → Copy to Clipboard.
2
In Just TCG, open the Decks tab, tap +, and choose Import Deck.
3
Tap Paste from Clipboard. The deck list is parsed automatically.
4
If any cards can't be matched, an Unmatched Cards sheet appears. For each unmatched card, pick the correct version from the catalog. Tap Import when done.

Import from file

Tap the document icon on the Import sheet to open a file picker and select a saved deck list text file.

Tip: You can also import a tournament deck list directly from the deck list viewer in the Tournaments tab. Tap Import Deck from any tournament deck list screen.

Scan cards with your camera

The Card Scanner uses Vision OCR to read the set symbol and collector number from a physical card's lower edge, then adds the matching card to your active deck.

1
Open your deck in Deck Detail, then tap the camera icon in the toolbar.
2
Hold the card so the set symbol (bottom left) and collector number (bottom right) are visible and in focus.
3
When the app recognises the card, a confirmation appears at the bottom. The card is added to the deck immediately.
4
Continue holding cards up to scan your whole physical deck.
Tip: Good lighting matters. Avoid direct sunlight glare on holographic cards. If scanning fails, try tilting the card slightly or moving to an area with better even lighting.

AI Deck Generator

Describe the deck you want in plain English and the generator will build a complete 60-card list using tournament-placed decks as a source.

1
Open the Decks tab and tap + → Generate Deck.
2
Type a description. For example: "A budget Gardevoir ex build that can handle Charizard" or "Fastest possible Regidrago VSTAR setup."
3
Tap Generate. The AI builds the deck list and displays it for review.
4
Tap Import Deck to save the generated list as an editable deck.
Requires: iOS 18.1+, A17 Pro chip or later, and Apple Intelligence enabled.

Consistency Calculator

The Consistency Calculator shows the probability of drawing specific cards in your opening hand.

1
In Deck Detail, tap the Consistency button (chart icon).
2
Select one or more cards from your deck. For each card, enter how many copies are in the deck (auto-filled) and how many you want in your opener.
3
The calculator uses hypergeometric distribution to show the probability of holding at least your target count in a 7-card opening hand from a 60-card deck.

Use this to evaluate whether your Pokémon search count or Energy ratio gives you the opening consistency you need.

Tech Advisor

The Tech Advisor recommends tech cards for your specific matchup problems.

1
In Deck Detail, tap the Tech Advisor button (wrench icon).
2
Describe your problem: "I keep losing to Iron Thorns because I can't set up fast enough" or "My Gardevoir matchup feels 50/50 — what can tilt it?"
3
The advisor suggests specific Standard-legal cards with a clear explanation of why each card addresses the problem.
Requires: iOS 18.1+, A17 Pro chip or later. Runs fully on-device — your query is never sent to a server.

Export a deck

Export any deck as a PTCGL-compatible text list.

1
Open the deck in Deck Detail.
2
Tap the Share button (square with arrow) in the top right.
3
Share to clipboard, Files, Messages, or any app. In PTCGL, use Deck → Import → Paste from Clipboard.

Log a match

1
Open the deck you played in Deck Detail.
2
Tap Log Match (+ button in the toolbar).
3
Type or select your opponent's archetype. The search box suggests from a curated list of current meta archetypes. You can also type any freeform archetype name.
4
Tap Win, Loss, or Tie.
5
Tap Log to save.

Date defaults to now. Event type defaults to the last event type you used. To add event type, format (BO1/BO3), or notes, tap More options before logging.

Faster: Use Live Game Mode before the game and the result auto-populates the log sheet when the game ends — you only need to confirm.

Live Game Mode

Track an active game in real time — prizes, opponent state, and turn notes.

1
From Deck Detail, tap Live Game (joystick icon) before the game starts.
2
Select the opponent's archetype on the setup sheet and tap Start Game.
3
During the game, use the HUD to track your prize count, opponent's prize count, and current turn. Tap Next Turn to advance, or add a turn note for anything you want to remember.
4
When the game ends, tap End Game and select the result. The match log sheet pre-fills with the opponent archetype — just confirm to save.

Match history

Each deck has a full match history accessible from Deck Detail → History tab. Matches are shown chronologically, grouped by deck version (based on your edit history). Tap any match to see the full details and any notes you added.

The history is segmented by deck version so you can see how card changes affected your results over time.

Matchup analytics

The Analytics tab shows win rate data for the selected deck.

  • Matchup Win Rates — Win/loss/tie counts and win rate % for every archetype you've faced, sorted by number of games.
  • Favourable / Unfavourable tags — Automatically applied when you have 5+ games against an archetype. Favourable = ≥60% win rate. Unfavourable = ≤40%.
  • Win Rate Trend — Chart of rolling win rate over time. Toggle between 30 days, 90 days, and all-time.
Sample size matters. A win rate based on 2 games is nearly meaningless. The app flags thin matchups so you know to treat them with caution.

Meta comparison

Meta Comparison cross-references your matchup data against the current tournament meta.

  • Practice Gaps — Top-meta archetypes where you have fewer than 5 games logged. You don't know your real win rate against these decks yet.
  • Danger Matchups — Top-meta archetypes where your logged win rate is below 40%. These are matchups that will likely cost you at a tournament if left unaddressed.

Meta share percentages are sourced from recent Limitless TCG tournament results and update automatically as new events are processed.

Meta Matchup Score

The Meta Matchup Score is a single number that summarises how well your deck is positioned against the current tournament field.

It weights your win rate against each archetype by that archetype's current meta share percentage. A score above 50 means you're beating the field on average; below 50 means the field is beating you.

The score updates automatically as the tournament meta shifts and as you log more games.

Tournament feed

The Tournaments tab shows recent major Pokémon TCG events sourced from Limitless TCG.

Each event row shows the event name, location, date, and player count. Tap an event to see the full results breakdown — player names, archetypes, and finishing positions for the top cut.

Tournament data is fetched fresh when you open the tab (with a 1-hour cache to avoid unnecessary requests). Pull to refresh for the latest data. Results are cached for offline reading.

Viewing tournament deck lists

Tap any placing in a tournament to view the full 60-card deck list inline. You can see the complete list — Pokémon, Trainers, Energy — with card thumbnails, without leaving the app.

From the deck list viewer, tap Import Deck to save the list as your own editable deck.

Meta trends

Meta Trends shows how each archetype's share of the tournament field has changed across recent events. Access it from the Tournaments tab.

Trend lines rise when a deck is gaining presence and fall when it's declining. Use this to:

  • Time when to pick up a rising deck
  • Identify when your current deck's good matchups are disappearing from the field
  • Spot format shifts early — a deck spiking into the top tier before most players adapt

Tap any archetype in the trends view to see its meta share percentage at each event and view recent top-placing deck lists.

Player profiles

Follow competitive players and track their tournament history.

1
Open the Competition tab (accessible from Home or Tournaments).
2
Search any player by name. Results are sourced from Limitless TCG's player database.
3
Tap a player to see their full tournament history — events, finishing positions, and deck lists.
4
Tap the star icon to add them to your Favourites. Favourite players appear at the top of the Competition tab for fast access.

Tournament Prep Planner

The Prep Planner helps you prepare systematically for an upcoming event by setting matchup goals and logging prep sessions.

Create a Prep Plan

1
Open the Prep tab (accessible from Home navigation).
2
Tap + New Plan. Name it (e.g. "Portland Regionals prep") and set a target date.
3
Tap Add Matchup Goal. Choose an archetype and a target win rate (e.g. "Charizard ex — 60% win rate").
4
Add as many goals as your prep plan needs.

Log prep sessions

1
From a Prep Plan, tap Log Session.
2
Select which matchup you practiced, enter the number of games, and add any notes.
3
The plan's progress bars update automatically based on your match log data for each goal archetype.

Rules Assistant

The Rules Assistant answers rules questions grounded in the official Pokémon TCG rulebook.

1
Tap the ? button in the top-right of the Home screen (or anywhere you see it in the nav bar).
2
Type your question. For example: "Can I use two Pokémon Tools on the same Pokémon?" or "What happens if both players draw their last prize at the same time?"
3
The assistant responds with a clear answer and cites the relevant rule from the rulebook.
Works offline. The Rules Assistant runs entirely on-device using Apple Foundation Models. No internet required, no query is sent anywhere. Requires iOS 18.1+ and a compatible device.

Settings

Access Settings via the gear icon in the Home screen navigation bar.

  • Streak goal — Set the number of games per day that counts as meeting your daily goal (1–10). Affects the streak counter and the Home widget.
  • Preview cards — Controls how many card thumbnails appear per deck in the Decks list (1, 2, or 3).
  • Lite mode — Disables the Featured Deck widget on Home to skip its network fetch. Enabled automatically on older devices. You can toggle it manually here.

Backup & Restore

Export a backup

1
Go to Settings → Data → Export Backup.
2
A JSON file is created containing all your decks and match history.
3
Use the iOS Share Sheet to save the file to Files, AirDrop it to another device, or send it anywhere.

Import a backup

1
Go to Settings → Data → Import Backup.
2
Select the backup JSON file.
3
A confirmation sheet shows how many decks and matches will be imported. Decks that already exist on the device (matched by ID) will be skipped.
4
Tap Import to confirm.
Tip: Export a backup before major iOS updates or before resetting your device. iCloud sync covers most cases, but a local backup is a reliable safety net.
